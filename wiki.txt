Morphine is a pain medication of the opiate family which is found
naturally in a number of plants and animals.
It acts directly on the central nervous system (CNS) to decrease the feeling of pain.
It can be taken for both acute pain and chronic pain.
It is frequently used for pain from myocardial infarction and during labor.
It can be given by mouth, by injection into a muscle, by injection under the skin, 
intravenously, injection into the space around the spinal cord, or rectally.
Maximum effect is reached after about 20 minutes when given intravenously
 and after 60 minutes when given by mouth, while duration of effect is 3–7 hours.
Long-acting formulations also exist.
